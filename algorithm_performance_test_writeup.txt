Title: 3.5 Algorithm Performance Test

This section presents a comparative analysis of the five candidate algorithms: Decision Tree, Random Forest, SVM, KNN, and Naive Bayes. The comparison integrates both predictive accuracy and computational efficiency to determine the most suitable model for long-term deployment.

Random Forest is the superior algorithm regarding predictive metrics (Accuracy: 95.83%, ROC-AUC: 0.985), making it the preferred choice for high-stakes risk identification. However, for environments with limited hardware, Decision Tree offers a compelling balance of high accuracy (91.67%) and significantly lower training time (2.45ms).

Naive Bayes and KNN, while efficient, exhibited lower precision and recall, suggesting they may overlook certain nuances in student behavior compared to ensemble methods.

Conclusion:
Random Forest is recommended as the primary classification engine, with Decision Tree serving as a high-speed alternative.
