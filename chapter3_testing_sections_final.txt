Title: 3.2 Accuracy Test

The accuracy test evaluates the predictive capability of the selected machine learning algorithms in identifying students at risk of academic failure. Using a binary classification approach (Good Standing vs. At-Risk), the models were tested against a set of key performance indicators: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC.

Random Forest emerged as the top-performing model with an accuracy of 95.83% and an F1-score of 0.96. The high ROC-AUC value of 0.985 indicates excellent class separation. Decision Tree followed closely with 91.67% accuracy, showing strong reliability for simple rule-based classification.

The Cross-Validation (CV) Mean scores confirm the stability of these results across different subsets of the student performance dataset, with Random Forest maintaining the highest consistency (CV Mean: 0.942).

Title: 3.4 Performance Test

The performance test focuses on the backend system efficiency and computational resource utilization of the predictive models. This assessment is critical for ensuring that the Early Warning System can provide real-time feedback without exhausting server resources.

Key metrics monitored include Training Execution Time, Prediction Latency, Peak Memory Usage, and CPU Utilization. Naive Bayes and KNN demonstrated the lowest computational footprint, with Naive Bayes requiring only 1.24ms for training and 8.5% CPU utilization. 

Random Forest, while highly accurate, required the most resources with a training time of 115.32ms and 24.8MB of memory usage. Despite this, all models stayed within acceptable bounds for an educational management system, with prediction latencies remaining below 0.2ms per record.

Title: 3.5 Algorithm Performance Test

This section presents a comparative analysis of the five candidate algorithms: Decision Tree, Random Forest, SVM, KNN, and Naive Bayes. The comparison integrates both predictive accuracy and computational efficiency to determine the most suitable model for long-term deployment.

Random Forest is the superior algorithm regarding predictive metrics (Accuracy: 95.83%, ROC-AUC: 0.985), making it the preferred choice for high-stakes risk identification. However, for environments with limited hardware, Decision Tree offers a compelling balance of high accuracy (91.67%) and significantly lower training time (2.45ms).

Naive Bayes and KNN, while efficient, exhibited lower precision and recall, suggesting they may overlook certain nuances in student behavior compared to ensemble methods.

Conclusion:
Random Forest is recommended as the primary classification engine, with Decision Tree serving as a high-speed alternative.
